What Women Want campaign in Kasese District

PADAFRICA participated in What Women Want campaign in Kasese District to improve and demand for quality maternal and reproductive healthcare for women and strengthen health system.
What Women Want is a global advocacy campaign to improve quality maternal and reproductive healthcare for women and girls and strengthen health systems. Launched on April 11, 2018—International Maternal Health and Rights Day
What Women Want sets out to query one million women and girls worldwide—from capital cities to rural villages—about their top priority for quality maternal and reproductive health services until the end of March 2019.

Based on Hamara Swasthya, Hamari Awaz (Our Health, Our Voices), a grassroots campaign designed in partnership with the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation, White Ribbon Alliance India mobilized over 150,000 women in 2017 through the efforts of 100+ White Ribbon Alliance members.
What Women Want aims to: 
1.Educate and empower individuals about the importance of quality, equity and dignity in women’s and girls’ healthcare
2.Support women and girls to demand access to high-quality and dignified care and
3.Place women’s and girls’ self-articulated needs at the center of health policies, programs and accountability.

VOLUNTEER

What Women Want will grow the global movement for quality healthcare for women and girls. At the same time, it will help governments, health professionals, private providers and civil society organizations better understand what is most important to women and girls when it comes to their healthcare and to push for change within countries and communities.

The findings will be aggregated for a global picture of what women and girls’ want and disaggregated by country. Findings will be distilled into global and national advocacy agendas that reflect the top healthcare “asks,” including recommendations for improving quality, equity and dignity for health. The campaign is exploring its ability to provide disaggregated results at the sub-national level to inform localized action as much as possible.

Over 800 women and girls die during pregnancy and childbirth every day. These needless deaths could be prevented through quality healthcare. Because quality healthcare means different things to different people, it is important to hear directly from women and girls about their priority for their own reproductive and maternal healthcare. Want to help us reach 1 million women and girls? Download the #WhatWomenWant social media toolkit and help promote the campaign by sharing the survey through your social media channels.

Hepatitis and HIV/AIDS awareness

While HIV/AIDS inflicts 7.2% of the Ugandan population, 10% of the some population lives with Hepatitis.
The co infliction of these two viruses has enormous effect on the live,
PADAFRICA has carried out community awareness campaigns, testing of Hepatitis and HIV/AIDS.
Currently, PADAFRICA is implementing a Hepatitis B Community Awareness Project in partnership with NOPLHB in districts of Kasese. The objective of the project is to screen for Hepatitis, raise awareness about Hepatitis B in the districts of Uganda and refer patients to specialized care and management. This project is being implemented through a series of basic educational lectures that are culturally and linguistically sensitive that provides practical information to the community and therefore helps them understand the Public Health threat that Hepatitis B represents.

Sexual reproductive Health in Adolescents

Every day in developing countries, 20,000 girls under age 18 give birth. This amounts to 7.3 million births a year. And if all pregnancies are included, not just births, the number of adolescent pregnancies are much higher.
Uganda has one of the highest fertility rates in the world at an average of 5.4[]. In Uganda, one in every four girls between the ages of 15 to 19 is pregnant or has had a child already. Studies show that a girl between the ages 15 to 19 is likely to die or get complications during child birth compared to a woman in her 20's.About 80% of the estimated causes of women suffering from obstetric fistula are teenage girls who gave birth before the age of 19[].
When a girl becomes pregnant, her life can change radically. Her education may end and her job prospects diminish. She becomes more vulnerable to poverty and exclusion, and her health often suffers. Complications from pregnancy and childbirth are the leading cause of death among adolescent girls.

VOLUNTEER

Adolescent pregnancy is generally not the result of a deliberate choice – these girls often have little say over decisions affecting their lives. Rather, early pregnancy is a consequence of little or no access to school, information or health care.
In Kasese, PADAFRICA focuses on protecting girls, prevent early union and challenge cultural practices and beliefs that promote early marriages. This includes supporting comprehensive sexuality education and sexual and reproductive health care to help girls avoid pregnancy in schools both Primary, Secondary and Tertiary institutions. PADAFRICA also supporting girls who become pregnant acquire hands on skills for self sustaining
